Co-op Fitness Dashboard

Shared meal prep & training strategies for Edmonton PhD students.

Rogelio (You) 38yo • 68 kg • 168 cm Goal: Chest/Abs Recomp
Ruilin Chinese • 95 kg • 185 cm Goal: Drop to 85kg

Dual Strategy Overview

You both have different bodies but can use the same kitchen. Rogelio needs ~2,000 kcal for body recomposition (building muscle while losing fat). Ruilin needs ~2,400 kcal. Because he is taller, heavier, and highly active (20 hrs/week in a restaurant), his metabolism burns more. Dropping from 95kg to 85kg is the perfect target for his 185cm height, bringing him into a healthy BMI range.

The "Shared Kitchen" Protocol

  • ✓ One Base Recipe, Two Plates: You will cook the same Pan-Asian meals (blending Filipino and Chinese profiles like soy, garlic, ginger, and rice). The only difference is the portion sizes on your plates.
  • ✓ Ruilin's Shift Fuel: Since he works in a restaurant, he must time his largest carb intake (rice) before his shift to give him energy for standing, minimizing snacking on restaurant food.
  • ✓ Shared Groceries: Buying eggs, rice, chicken breast, and broccoli in bulk at Costco will cut your grocery bills by 30-40%.

Projected 12-Week Progress

Ruilin steadily drops weight; Rogelio maintains weight but drops body fat.

Ideal PhD Daily Schedule

Structure is the key to balancing a PhD and fitness. Switch between your focused weekday routine, your Saturday recovery, and your Sunday reset.

07:00 AM

Wake Up & Prime

Drink 500ml of water immediately. Light stretching. Review today's top 3 priorities (1 for PhD, 1 for Fitness, 1 for Life).

07:30 AM

Breakfast

High protein breakfast to break the fast. Coffee or Tea.

08:30 AM

Deep Academic Work

Paper Writing & Literature Review. Your brain is sharpest in the morning. Do the hardest cognitive tasks before checking emails or dealing with lab chaos. Block out distractions.

10:30 AM

Experiments & Lab Work

Transition to the lab bench or technical setup. Run your primary experiments for the day.

12:30 PM

Lunch Break

Eat your pre-packed lunch from yesterday's prep. Step outside the lab to decompress.

01:30 PM

Data, Meetings & Admin

Analyze morning experiment data. Attend meetings with your PI or research group. Answer emails.

05:00 PM

Gym / Workout

Leave the lab mentally behind. Head to the gym for your 60-minute workout. Let out the academic stress on the weights.

07:00 PM

Co-op Cooking & Dinner

Team up with Ruilin in the kitchen. Cook the base meal, divide into your specific portions. Pack the leftover portions into Tupperware for tomorrow's lunch immediately.

08:30 PM

Flex Time / Social

Free time. Call family in the Philippines, watch a show, play games, or do light reading. No heavy PhD work allowed here to prevent burnout.

11:00 PM

Wind Down & Sleep

Screen curfew. Read a non-academic book. Try to be asleep by 12:00 MN to ensure a solid 7 hours of recovery for your brain and muscles.

08:30 AM

Sleep In & Recover

Your body grows muscle while you sleep. Take the extra 1.5 hours to recover from the week's intense training and academic stress.

09:30 AM

Big Breakfast

Enjoy a slower morning. Make something more elaborate like Protein Pancakes or a large omelette. Coordinate with Ruilin.

11:00 AM

Hobbies & Flex Time

Do things completely unrelated to your PhD. Play video games, read for pleasure, or just relax. Only use this time for PhD work if you are strictly behind on a critical deadline.

01:30 PM

Lunch

Light lunch (e.g., leftovers or a wrap) to fuel your afternoon activities.

03:00 PM

Active Recovery / Sports

Play badminton with Ruilin, join a pickup basketball game, or go for a long walk in the Edmonton River Valley. Move for fun, not for reps.

06:30 PM

Dinner / Cheat Meal & Social

This is your psychological break. Go out to eat with friends, order takeout, and don't worry excessively about macros for this one meal.

11:30 PM

Sleep

Head to bed to rest up for Sunday's prep day.

08:00 AM

Wake Up

Start the day slightly earlier to beat the crowds for errands.

08:30 AM

Quick Breakfast

Oatmeal or a protein shake. Keep it light and fast.

09:30 AM

Grocery Run (Costco)

Hit up Costco/Superstore as early as possible with Ruilin. Buy all bulk proteins, rice, eggs, and veggies for the week before the afternoon rush.

11:30 AM

Cleaning & Laundry

Start the washing machine (gym clothes and lab clothes). Vacuum, clean the kitchen counters, and reset the apartment for a fresh week.

01:30 PM

Lunch

Eat a quick lunch using ingredients you just bought.

02:30 PM

Batch Meal Prep

Cook the large batch of rice in the rice cooker. Pre-chop vegetables and store them in containers. Marinate chicken/beef for Mon-Wed so dinners take only 15 minutes to cook during the week.

06:00 PM

Dinner & Relax

Eat a healthy, home-cooked meal using your freshly prepped food.

08:30 PM

Weekly Review & Planning

Sit down for 20 minutes. Review what experiments you need to run this week, what papers you need to read, and confirm your gym schedule. A planned week reduces PhD stress.

10:30 PM

Wind Down & Sleep

Go to bed early to reset your circadian rhythm for Monday morning at the lab.
